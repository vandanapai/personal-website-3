Boost Healthcare Consulting

Boost’s website was stuck in 1998, and they were concerned with the offhand comments they were receiving from clients about it. They needed a major refresh in order to prove their credibility as a consultancy and demonstrate to clients their professionalism. For 3 months, I worked closely with the client to redesign and develop their website. The result was a responsive website that was modern and sophisticated, bringing in more business and interested job applicants.

Type

Web

Role

Freelance web designer and developer

Tools

HTML5, SCSS, Javascript/jQuery

Process

Conducting User Research

Getting an understanding of the people at Boost was my first task. It was important to me to understand the vibe of the team, what everyone wanted the website to achieve, and the main message they wanted to get across. We went through many thought questions like “How would you define Boost?”, “How do you imagine people to use your website?” and “How do you want users to feel upon first visiting your website?”.

Creating Personas and Interpreting Feedback

During the discovery period, I uncovered the two primary goals Boost wanted to accomplish with their website: to present the company in a professional way that establishes trust with prospective clients, and to also attract potential new employees. The two main users were students and seasoned healthcare professionals, yet both had to feel “impressed and hopeful” upon visiting the site.

Wireframes

I first did a working session with the client to do initial sketches together. This was helpful to see their vision, and also inform them as to best practices, what’s feasible, and give them the best recommendation. We worked together to define what pages were necessary, and how each one would solve a specific need. For healthcare professionals, there are defined sections of Boost's expertise and services. For prospective employees, we included areas that highlighted company culture, activities, employee profiles.

Aesthetic

Recreating an Identity

The design challenge was to define an aesthetic that was corporate, but not too stuffy. Building on their existing brand, I revamped the logo, colors, and typography to push them into the modern era.

A slight tweak of the logo from multi-dimensional to flat and white helped to lose the gaudyness and bring some sophistication.

A bold Boost orange was the perfect representation of the vibrant and energetic team. To complement it, we chose a sleek navy blue to establish a sense of trust, strength, and reliability. This also set up great contrast for action items on the site.

Iconography

I chose to mix line iconography with some subtle animations to give the website a contemporary and playful spirit. Each icon is an SVG, so they could easily be manipulated and animated with CSS.
